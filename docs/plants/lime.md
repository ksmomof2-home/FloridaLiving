# Key / Persian Lime
**Citrus × aurantiifolia** | Rutaceae

<img src ="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ksmomof2-home/FloridaLiving/main/docs/assets/lime.jpg width=400 alt = "Lime Tree">

### Quick Facts
- USDA zones: 9–11 (protect below 28 °F)
- Sun: Full sun
- Water: Average — never let it sit bone-dry
- Mature height in 30-gal pot: 6–10 ft
- Fruit: Year-round in 9b with good care

### Primary Use
- Culinary → fresh lime juice, zest, mojitos!

### Wildlife Supported
- Nectar for bees when flowering
- Occasional giant swallowtail host (rare in pots)

### Pests to Watch
- Citrus leafminer → cosmetic only, ignore
- Whiteflies → soap spray if bad
- Scale → wipe with alcohol

### Unusual / Fun Facts
- Thornless varieties exist — yours may have small thorns
- Flowers smell incredible

### Local Care Tips (The Villages 9b)
- Fertilize every 6–8 weeks with citrus food
- Add crushed egg shells (loves calcium)
- Wrap trunk + frost cloth + Christmas lights below 30 °F
