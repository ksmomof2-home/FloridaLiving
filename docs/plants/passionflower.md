# Passionflower (Maypop)
**Passiflora incarnata** | Passifloraceae

<img src = "https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ksmomof2-home/FloridaLiving/main/docs/assets/passionflower.jpg?" width=400 alt = "Maypop flower">

### Quick Facts
- USDA zones: 5–10 (native Florida wildflower!)
- Sun: Full sun to part shade
- Water: Average to moist
- Mature height in pot: 6–15 ft vine (climbs fence)
- Days to bloom: 60–90 from transplant

### Primary Use
- Butterfly host → **only food for Gulf fritillary caterpillars in Florida**

### Wildlife Supported
- Host for: Gulf fritillary (orange spiky cats!)
- Nectar for: bees, butterflies, hummingbirds

### Pests to Watch
- Gulf fritillary caterpillars → **DO NOT KILL** — they are the point!
- Whiteflies → ignore passionflower

### Unusual / Fun Facts
- The fruit (maypop) is edible when yellow and wrinkly — tastes like tropical bubblegum
- Used by Native Americans as a calming tea

### Local Care Tips (The Villages 9b)
- Let it climb the fence — no trellis needed
- Dies back in winter, explodes in spring
- Current status: Pos 3 — fertilized, foiled, mulched Nov 20
