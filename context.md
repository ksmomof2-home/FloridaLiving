## 3. Recurring Garden Schedule

### Daily (5–10 minutes)
- Check for hornworms & whiteflies → hand-pick or spray at dusk if needed
- Water new transplants deeply if no rain
- Quick deadhead/inspect for caterpillars (enjoy them!)

### Weekly
- Mix & apply butterfly-safe whitefly spray (dusk) if population is high
- Water established pots deeply 2–3× week (more in spring)
- Check sticky traps, replace when full
- Harvest cherry tomatoes to keep plant producing

### Monthly
- Fertilize everything with 6-6-6 or fish emulsion (half-strength on herbs)
- Side-dress tomatoes with compost mid-month
- Inspect trellises/cages, tighten ties
- Release ladybugs if whitefly pressure spikes (dusk)

### Quarterly (Mar, Jun, Sep, Dec)
- Deep clean pots & refresh top 2–3" of soil
- Re-mulch
- Check & repair shade cloth/frost cloth

### Yearly
- Feb/Mar: Prune avocado & lime trees
- Apr/May: Set up 30% shade cloth for summer
- Oct: Plant winter tomatoes & cool-season herbs
- Nov: Prep frost protection kit

## 4. Frost & Freeze Protection Plan (Nov–Feb)

Temperatures → Action
38–45°F → Water soil well day before (insulates roots)
32–38°F → Cover pots with frost cloth + mulch heavily
28–32°F → Frost cloth + Christmas lights underneath + wrap trunks of citrus/avocado
<28°F → Double layer frost cloth + lights + move smallest pots into garage overnight

Supplies to keep ready in garage:
- Frost cloth (at least 20×20 ft pieces)
- C9 Christmas lights or 100-ft rope light
- Clothespins & stakes
- Cardboard boxes or old sheets as extra layer

## 5. Pest Control – Butterfly & Bee Safe Only

Whitefly primary recipe (repeat every 3–4 days until gone)
- 1 gal warm water
- 2 Tbsp Dr. Bronner’s Castile soap
- 1 Tbsp cold-pressed neem OR horticultural soap
- 1 Tbsp peppermint essential oil (optional turbo)
→ Spray at dusk, undersides especially. Dries harmless.

Other safe tools
- Yellow sticky traps
- Reflective mulch or aluminum foil under plants
- Ladybug releases (dusk)
- BT (Thuricide) only on hornworms you don’t want (won’t hurt swallowtail/monarch cats)

**NEVER spray neem oil or azadirachtin products on milkweed, parsley, dill, or fennel.**
